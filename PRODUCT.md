# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small business owners (clinics, trades, local services) who need a professional web presence without hiring a developer. They open Landora between client work: a short session to update copy, swap a photo, publish a blog post, or check that their domain is live. They are not designers or engineers; they want the tool to feel obvious, not like a site builder control room.

## Product Purpose

Landora is a multi-tenant SaaS for creating and managing landing pages. Business owners edit structured content (hero, services, testimonials, blog, SEO) through a focused dashboard; changes publish to their custom domain or subdomain. Success means a non-technical user completes an edit and publishes with confidence, without support or documentation.

## Brand Personality

Confident and capable. Plain language over jargon. The product should feel like a serious tool that respects the user's time: calm hierarchy, clear next steps, no hand-holding theater. Reference feel: Notion and Linear (clean product UI, subtle depth, efficient layouts).

## Anti-references

- Generic AI SaaS: cream or purple-gradient dashboards, eyebrow labels on every section, ghost-card borders with wide drop shadows, decorative motion for its own sake.
- Overwhelming site builders: dense sidebars, simultaneous panels, icon grids of equal-weight options, builder jargon ("blocks", "widgets", "modules") when simpler words work.
- Cold enterprise admin: gray-on-gray tables, unexplained status badges, actions hidden behind menus.

## Design Principles

1. **One obvious next step** — Every screen answers "what do I do here?" without scanning five zones. Primary action is visible; secondary actions stay reachable but quiet.
2. **Edit like content, not configuration** — Fields read as documents (title, excerpt, body) before they read as CMS metadata. Reduce the feeling of operating machinery.
3. **Progressive disclosure** — Show what most sessions need first (edit, preview, publish). Advanced settings (domain, SEO detail, admin) appear when the user goes looking, not by default.
4. **Confidence through state** — Draft vs published, saved vs saving, connected vs pending domain: status is always readable. Never leave the user guessing whether their work is live.
5. **Professional, not intimidating** — Capable and trustworthy without corporate coldness. Success is the user thinking "I can handle this" after the first session.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for the product UI: keyboard operability, visible focus, sufficient contrast on body and control labels, meaningful link and button text. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Public landing templates follow the same baseline but may be tuned per template later.
